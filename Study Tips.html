Study Tips for Sri Lankan Students

1. Make a Study Plan

Follow your school timetable or make a subject rotation.

Study Plan/Guide
Step What To Do
1.Gather resources Get your school textbooks in your exam medium (Sinhala, Tamil or English). Use the National Institute of Education (NIE) books, plus past papers. Marking schemes help.
2.Past papers Practice past papers from previous years. Try them under exam conditions (timed) to get used to time pressure. Review marking schemes to see what examiners look for.
3.Subject wise strategy For each subject, do the following:
  • Science / Maths: Practice problems and numerical questions; understand practicals.
  • Languages (Sinhala/Tamil/English): grammar, comprehension, writing.
  • History / Geography / Civics: remember facts, dates, map work.
  • ICT / Technical subjects: practice using the tools/software as required, do practical tasks.
4.Time Management Make a weekly study schedule. Allocate more time for weak subjects. Include revision slots. Leave enough time before exam for full reviews and mock exams.
5. Use marking schemes Go through marking schemes (like the Science Sinhala Medium 2025 scheme) to understand how marks are allocated, what keywords are needed. (Examlanka)
6. Learn exam formats Know how many sections per subject, number of questions, time limits, how many questions you must answer.
7. Improve writing and presentation Answers should be clear, neat, structured. Use good handwriting. If maps or diagrams needed, draw them properly. Label them well.
8. Revision Make short notes of important points. Use flashcards for facts, terminologies. Quiz yourself or with friends.
9. Past exam tips Previous students say repetition of past paper questions, drilling map marking, reading widely for essay/written sections helps a lot.

2. Practice Past Papers

Use Pastpapers.wiki or e-Thaksalawa.

3. Use Video Lessons

Nenasa TV or Channel NIE for local-language support.

4. Offline Access

Download PDFs and videos where possible for low-data usage.
